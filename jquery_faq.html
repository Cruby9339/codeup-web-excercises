10 Faqs about national parks

Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Shawn Benge
What Government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.
How many employees are in the National Park Service
Approximately 20,000
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The components of the arrowhead may have been inspired by key attributes of the National Park System, with the sequoia tree and bison representing vegetation and wildlife, the mountains and water representing scenic and recreational values, and the arrowhead itself representing historical and archeological values.
How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2019: 327,516,619.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 423 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres
What is the smallest national park site?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres
What is the most-visited national park?
Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Big Bend National Park

  • Big Bend National Park covers a total area of 801,163 acres
  • The number of people visiting Big Bend in 2019 was 463,832
  • Big Bend was made a national park on June 20, 1935
  • The highest elevation found in Big Bend is 7,832 feet on Emory Peak

Joshua Tree National Park

  • Joshua Tree National Park covers a total area of 789,745 acres.
  • The number of people visiting Joshua Tree in 2019 was 2,988,547
  • Joshua Tree was made a national park on October 31, 1994.
  • The lowest elevation found in Joshua Tree is 934 feet at Pinto Wells.

Redwood State Park

  • Redwood National Park covers a total area of 131,983 acres
  • The number of people visiting Redwood in 2019 was 504,722
  • Redwood was made a national park on October 2, 1968
  • The average lifespan of a coast redwood is 500-700 years, though they have been known to exceed 2,000 years in age.
